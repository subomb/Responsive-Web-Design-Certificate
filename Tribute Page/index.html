Bill Gates

Bill Gates, co-founder of Microsoft Corporation, has significantly influenced the personal computing revolution. As a leading entrepreneur and visionary, Gates transformed technology to be accessible for everyday use. His contributions extend into global philanthropy through the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, addressing pressing issues like health and education. Gates continues to inspire future innovation and impact lives around the world.

Biography

  • 1955 - Born as William Henry Gates III in Seattle, Washington, on October 28.
  • 1973 - Graduates from Lakeside School; scores 1590 out of 1600 on the SAT; enrolls at Harvard College.
  • 1975 - Drops out of Harvard and co-founds Microsoft with Paul Allen.The company is dedicated to developing software for the first microcomputers.
  • 1980 - Microsoft signs a contract with IBM to provide an operating system for their first personal computer, leading to the development of MS-DOS.
  • 1985 - Microsoft launches Windows 1.0, introducing a graphical user interface (GUI) to the PC market.
  • 1987 - Becomes the world's youngest billionaire at age 31.
  • 1995 - Launches Windows 95, solidifying Microsoft's market dominance.
  • 2000 - Steps down as CEO of Microsoft to focus more on philanthropy, forming the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, one of the world's largest private charitable foundations.
  • 2008 - Transitions away from a day-to-day role at Microsoft to spend more time on his philanthropic efforts, including global health, education, and access to information technology.
  • 2014 - Steps down as chairman of Microsoft but remains on the board and becomes a technology advisor to the new CEO, Satya Nadella..
  • 2020 - Leaves his board positions at Microsoft and Berkshire Hathaway to focus entirely on philanthropic activities.
“It's fine to celebrate success, but it is more important to heed the lessons of failure.” - Bill Gates
